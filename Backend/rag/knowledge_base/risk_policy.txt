RETIREMENT PLANNING GUIDELINES

Clients above age 60 should prioritize capital preservation and stable income generation.

Retirement portfolios should generally include a mix of equities, fixed income securities, and cash equivalents.

Clients nearing retirement should gradually reduce exposure to high-volatility assets.

Dividend-paying stocks and government bonds are preferred for conservative retirement portfolios.

Emergency liquidity covering at least 12 months of expenses is recommended for retirees.

SYSTEMATIC INVESTMENT AND WITHDRAWAL RULES

Systematic withdrawal plans should avoid excessive withdrawals during market downturns.

A withdrawal rate above 5% annually may increase longevity risk for retirement portfolios.

Dollar-cost averaging can reduce timing risk during volatile market periods.

RISK MANAGEMENT PRINCIPLES

High exposure to a single sector increases concentration risk.

Technology-heavy portfolios may experience elevated volatility during market corrections.

Diversification across equities, bonds, commodities, and international assets reduces portfolio risk.

Lower-risk investors should prioritize capital protection over aggressive growth.

Aggressive portfolios are suitable only for investors with high risk tolerance and long investment horizons.

FIXED INCOME STRATEGIES

Government bonds are considered lower-risk investments.

Corporate bonds may provide higher yield but carry credit risk.

Short-duration bonds are less sensitive to interest rate changes.

Bond ladders can help manage reinvestment risk.

PORTFOLIO REBALANCING

Portfolios should be reviewed and rebalanced periodically.

Annual rebalancing helps maintain target asset allocation.

Significant market movements may require tactical rebalancing.

TAX-AWARE INVESTING

Tax-efficient investing strategies can improve long-term returns.

Long-term capital gains are generally more tax-efficient than short-term gains.

Tax-loss harvesting may reduce taxable investment gains.

COMPLIANCE AND SUITABILITY

Financial recommendations must align with client risk tolerance and investment objectives.

Advisors should document suitability assessments before recommending aggressive investments.

High-risk products should not be recommended to conservative investors without proper disclosures.

Compliance reviews are required for portfolios exceeding defined risk thresholds.

CLIENT RISK PROFILES

Conservative investors prioritize capital preservation and stable income.

Moderate investors seek balanced growth and moderate risk exposure.

Aggressive investors prioritize long-term capital appreciation and can tolerate significant volatility.

Young investors with long investment horizons may tolerate higher equity exposure.

Retirees generally require lower portfolio volatility and higher income stability.
